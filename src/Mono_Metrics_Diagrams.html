Mono — Metrics Visual Reference

Every metric explained with diagrams · For Mayank Tyagi · Mono v2.0

📐 Section 1 — Flat Size Metrics

Flat Width & Flat Height

The dimensions of the carton when completely unfolded flat. This is what occupies space on the print sheet.
Flat W = 2 × (L + W) + Glue Flap (10mm)
Flat H = H + Top Tuck Depth + Bottom Flap Depth
Musli Power: L=45, W=45, H=83mm (Bottom Side Lock)
Flat W = 2×(45+45)+10 = 190mm
Flat H = 83+33.2+41.5 = 157.7mm

Flap Depths by Box Style

Each box style has different ratios for top tuck and bottom flap depths, calculated as a percentage of the carton height H.
H=83mm, Bottom Side Lock:
Top Tuck = 83×0.40 = 33.2mm
Bottom Lock = 83×0.50 = 41.5mm
Glue Flap = 10mm (standard)
📊 Section 2 — Layout Metrics

Cartons Per Sheet & Sheet Utilization

How many cartons fit on the usable area of the sheet, and what percentage of the sheet area is covered.
Usable W = Sheet W − 2 × Margin
Usable H = Sheet H − 2 × Margin
Cols = FLOOR(Usable W / Flat W)
Rows = FLOOR(Usable H / Flat H)
Cartons = Cols × Rows
Utilization = (Cartons × Flat W × Flat H) / (Usable W × Usable H) × 100
≥ 80% Excellent 60–80% Good < 60% Review

Nesting Saving (Tumble Layout)

In tumble layout, alternate rows are flipped 180°. The top tuck of one row nests beside the bottom of the adjacent flipped row, saving vertical space.
Nesting Saving mm = Top Tuck Depth
Nesting Saving % = Top Tuck / Flat H × 100
Pair Height = 2 × Flat H − Nesting Saving mm
Extra Rows = FLOOR(Usable H / Pair Height) × 2
Musli Power: Flat H=157.7mm, Nesting=33.2mm
Straight: 2 rows = 315.4mm → 6 rows in 980mm → 18 cartons
Tumble: pair = 282.2mm → 3 pairs+1 row in 980mm → 21 cartons
🖨️ Section 3 — Gang Printing Metrics

Impressions Needed & Overrun

Impressions = number of times the sheet goes through the press. Since you can only print complete sheets, you always round UP — which causes a small overrun.
Impressions = CEILING(Quantity / Cartons Per Sheet)
Actual Printed = Impressions × Cartons Per Sheet
Overrun % = (Actual − Required) / Required × 100
0–5% Acceptable 5–10% Review > 10% High waste

Impression Alignment Score

When multiple jobs are on the same sheet, they must all complete in the same number of impressions. Otherwise the second job is printed in a different press run — with different ink density — causing colour variation.
Score = 1 − (MAX impressions − MIN impressions) / MAX impressions
Score = 1.0 (100%) = perfect alignment = one press run
Score < 0.9 = colour consistency risk
100% Perfect 90–99% Acceptable < 90% Colour risk

Max Overrun % (Multi-SKU)

In a multi-SKU layout with multiple jobs, each job may have a different overrun. Max Overrun is the worst case across all jobs — Mono flags this if it exceeds your tolerance.
Max Overrun % = MAX(Overrun % across all jobs in plan)
Default tolerance = 5%
Warning shown if any job overrun > tolerance
0–5% Acceptable 5–10% Warning shown > 10% Review plan

Compatibility — Hard vs Soft Constraints

Before combining jobs on one sheet, Mono checks if they are compatible. Hard constraints BLOCK the combination. Soft constraints show warnings but don't block.
Hard constraint fail example:
Job A: FBB 330gsm UV + Job B: ITC Safire 350gsm UV
Paper mismatch → ❌ INCOMPATIBLE → separate sheets required
